ABOUT US

We are two researchers and VR developers based at two different labs in Germany. We first began working together during a hackathon, where we quickly realized how naturally our skills and approaches complemented one another. What started as a short collaboration turned into a shared vision: using VR technologies to make medical concepts more intuitive, visual and emotionally engaging.
Although we come from different labs in Germany, our backgrounds in computer science, mixed reality, medical applications and human–robot interaction give us a strong foundation for building experiences that go far beyond our individual research topics. Together, we explore new ways to teach, communicate and experience what happens inside the human body.

Ana Sofia Santos

Ana is an associate researcher at the Institute for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine (IKIM), University of Duisburg-Essen, working at the intersection of VR technology, medical imaging and Digital Humans. She is building conversational virtual assistants powered by speech recognition, retrieval-augmented generation and real-time avatar animation to improve how patients and learners access medical information.

Katarzyna Olszyńska

Katarzyna is an associate researcher at Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg. She focuses on human–robot interaction, mixed reality and interactive systems that support users in medical and rehabilitation settings.

Collaborative Work

We are currently collaborating on research-driven VR projects such as CellCrush. Our goal is to design serious games that are scientifically grounded, emotionally engaging and accessible to both healthcare professionals and patients.

Beyond this project, we are currently exploring game concepts like bloodstream navigation and interactive visualizations of treatment mechanisms, so that we can scale our initial concept and provide users with immersive and educational games that can empower and provide health literacy.
