CellCrush was born during the XRAI Hackathon by SenAI Hackademy in Cologne, August 2025. Our team works in XR development for healthcare and we keep noticing a persistent gap: XR technologies are transforming how medical professionals learn and train, however, patients are still left with pamphlets and explanations that feel abstract, overwhelming, or frightening. We wanted to change that.

CellCrush began as an experiment in creating a VR experience for people facing complex diseases. The idea was simple:

  • What if patients could see what is happening inside their bodies?
  • What if they could feel like they were actively fighting back?
  • What if education could be playful, visual, and empowering instead of stressful?

The result was a game where players explore a stylised bloodstream, battle harmful cells and learn how treatments work in a way that makes biology less intimidating. While the concept started with patients in mind, we quickly realised it has value far beyond clinical settings.

Families, students and anyone curious about the human body can explore CellCrush together. It‚Äôs both a science game and an empowerment tool designed to make learning about health engaging, social and accessible for all.

üöÄ Scalability & Vision for the Future

CellCrush was designed from the very beginning as a modular and expandable VR experience. The bloodstream is only our starting point. Our goal is to grow CellCrush into a vibrant, interconnected universe where users can explore how the human body works across multiple systems.

We are currently developing an expanded visual style and more immersive environments to make each ‚Äúworld inside the body‚Äù dynamic, colourful and scientifically meaningful. Future modules will allow players to follow oxygen molecules as they travel through the circulatory system, understand how wounds are repaired by platelets, see how immune cells respond to infection, or experience how insulin regulates blood glucose in conditions such as diabetes.

Each new world will build on the same core principles that shaped the original concept: clear science, interactive learning and a sense of agency.

By transforming complex physiology into playful, explorable environments, CellCrush aims to support patients in understanding their health, while providing engaging educational tools for families, students and curious learners.

Our long-term vision is to create the first VR patient-education ecosystem where every major body system becomes a world to explore, compare and understand. As we expand, we aim to collaborate with professionals to ensure scientific accuracy, clinical relevance and meaningful real-world impact.

CellCrush is not just a game; it is the foundation of a future in which health literacy is immersive, accessible and empowering for everyone.
