We are two researchers and XR developers based at Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg, Germany. Our shared work focuses on creating immersive healthcare experiences that help people understand complex medical concepts through interactive, spatial computing technologies.

Combining backgrounds in computer science, mixed reality, human–robot interaction and medical applications, we prototype new ways to teach, communicate and explore what happens inside the human body.

Ana Sofia Santos

Ana is an associate researcher at Otto-von-Guericke University Magdeburg. Her work sits at the intersection of mixed reality, medical imaging and digital humans for healthcare education and patient empowerment.

How We Work Together

We collaborate on research-driven XR projects such as CellCrush, a series of immersive micro-worlds inside the human body. Our goal is to design experiences that are scientifically grounded, emotionally engaging and accessible to both healthcare professionals and patients.

Beyond this project, we explore concepts like bloodstream navigation, interactive visualizations of treatment mechanisms, and conversational digital humans that can guide users through complex medical information.
